Project Overview

Streaming services have been critical in uncovering tertiary user television needs. Albeit, streaming organizations are quite savvy with new tools they have yet to present one that capitalizes on a market filled with those that appreciate the experience through a proxy. 'BingeBuddies' solves this need by connecting users watching the same program and/or movie, and, enabling them to effortlessly create and share (communal) their reactions, or, even, explore others reactions.

The Problem

Platforms for viewing shows and movies (i.e. streaming services) have funneled users into the same viewing experience that is highly non-interactive and typical. Users cannot fully enjoy or ‘delight’ in the viewing experience because of the absence of administration over categories that appear on their feed and, to an even greater degree, lack of tools that let them interact with (or create) content.

The Goal

The objective of this project was to provide streaming service matured users with greater interactivity and/or authority in regards to how content is organized and shown, as well as, created. We’re giving the floor to all those cinema commentators and instant feedback friends.

Pain Points

The inability to control what content appears on feeds and in categories, and, the categories themselves.

No easy way to join to and/or access what other friends and family are watching.

Little freedom with how content is categorized and displayed, and even less with how viewers can respond to content. Texts reviews can be the only way.

The review system is quite skewed. Poor reviews can lead to a common entertainment pet peeve: wasting time.

Conclusions

Lessons Learned

  • The growing sophistication of streaming audiences is enhancing how they want to interact with content especially in the context of a growing communal facet to digital spaces, and, this interest must be addressed
  • Users already enjoy efficient platforms, and, this experience must be coupled with an easy to navigate feature selection.

Next Steps

After reviewing a moderate number of like apps, and, having sufficient familiarity with existing (linked) streaming services there is a critical gap in what they offer those with accessibility needs. Next step may include changes to resolution, color, text size and font choice to meet those interests.
Also, we considered finding ways to grow memberships through randomized viewing groups.
